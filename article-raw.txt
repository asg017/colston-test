On August 23, 2020, '''Jacob Blake''', a 29-year-old black man, was shot from behind seven times by a police officer in [[Kenosha, Wisconsin]].<ref name="thedailybeast">{{cite web |last1=Connor |first1=Tracy |title=Wisconsin Police Shoot Black Man as Heâ€™s Getting in Car |url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/kenosha-wisconsin-police-shoot-black-man-as-hes-getting-in-car |publisher=[[The Daily Beast]] |date=August 24, 2020|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref name="thewashingtonpost">{{cite web |last1=Peiser |first1=Jaclyn |title=Video shows Wisconsin police shooting a Black man multiple times as he enters a car |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2020/08/23/kenosha-police-shooting-video-wisconsin/ |publisher=[[The Washington Post]] |date=August 24, 2020 |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Protests occurred in response, as part of the larger [[Black Lives Matter]] movement which had seen a resurgence in the wake of high-profile police killings in 2020, including [[Killing of George Floyd|George Floyd]] and [[Shooting of Breonna Taylor|Breonna Taylor]].

==Reactions==
Protests followed, leading the [[Kenosha County, Wisconsin|Kenosha County]] government to declare a [[State of emergency#United States|state of emergency]] overnight on August 24 after police vehicles were damaged, a dump truck was set on fire, looting occurred, and the local courthouse was vandalized.  One officer was hit in the head with a flying object, and tear gas was deployed.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2020/aug/24/protests-in-wisconsin-after-video-appears-to-show-police-shooting-black-man-in-the-back |title=Protests in Wisconsin after video appears to show police shooting black man in the back|date=August 24, 2020|publisher=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | last = Palmer | first = Ewan | date = August 24, 2020 | title = Rioting in Kenosha Over Jacob Blake Shooting, State of Emergency Curfew Declared | url = https://www.newsweek.com/jacob-blake-police-shooting-riots-kenosha-1527086 | work = Newsweek|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://jsonline.com/story/news/2020/08/24/kenosha-protests-escalate-after-police-shoot-black-man-jacob-blake/3427941001/ |title=Several fires burning in Kenosha as unrest continues after police officer shoots Black man |date=August 24, 2020|publisher= [[Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref name="npr"/> Police urged 24-hour businesses to consider closing because of what it alleged were "numerous" calls about armed robberies and shots being fired.<ref name=bbc/>

Wisconsin Governor [[Tony Evers]] issued a statement denouncing the [[Police brutality in the United States|excessive use of force by police]] and invoking the names of African Americans killed by law enforcement.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cbs58.com/news/gov-evers-releases-statement-on-shooting-of-jacob-blake-in-kenosha |title=Gov. Evers releases statement on shooting of Jacob Blake in Kenosha |date=August 23, 2020| publisher=[[WDJT-TV]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Evers stated that,
<blockquote>While we do not have all of the details yet, what we know for certain is that he is not the first Black man or person to have been shot or injured or mercilessly killed at the hands of individuals in law enforcement in our state or our country.<ref name=bbc>{{cite news|url= https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-53886070|work=[[BBC News]]|department=US & Canada|title=Kenosha shooting: Protests erupt after US police shoot black man|date=August 24, 2020|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref></blockquote>

Presidential candidate [[Joe Biden]] said, "these shots pierce the soul of our nation".<ref>{{cite news | last = Radcliff | first = J.R. | date = August 24, 2020 | title = Prominent Figures React to the Jacob Blake Shooting in Kenosha, Including Joe Biden | url = https://www.jsonline.com/story/news/2020/08/24/prominent-figures-react-jacob-blake-shooting-kenosha/3428435001/ | work = Milwaukee Journal Sentinal|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref>

The family of Jacob Blake has retained civil rights attorney [[Benjamin Crump]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/local/crime-and-courts/jacob-blakes-family-has-retained-prominent-civil-rights-attorney-ben-crump/article_d8455df7-34ce-5d3e-8571-82b3ce4432c0.html |title=Jacob Blake's family has retained prominent Civil Rights Attorney Ben Crump |date=August 24, 2020 |first=Adam |last=Rogan}}</ref>
