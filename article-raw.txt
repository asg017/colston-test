On August 23, 2020, Jacob S. Blake, a 29-year-old American black man, was shot from behind seven times by a police officer in [[Kenosha, Wisconsin]] United States.<ref name="thedailybeast">{{cite web |last1=Connor |first1=Tracy |title=Wisconsin Police Shoot Black Man as He’s Getting in Car |url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/kenosha-wisconsin-police-shoot-black-man-as-hes-getting-in-car |publisher=[[The Daily Beast]] |date=August 24, 2020|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref name="thewashingtonpost">{{cite web |last1=Peiser |first1=Jaclyn |title=Video shows Wisconsin police shooting a Black man multiple times as he enters a car |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2020/08/23/kenosha-police-shooting-video-wisconsin/ |publisher=[[The Washington Post]] |date=August 24, 2020 |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Police were attempting to arrest Blake, who, according to the Blake family attorney [[Benjamin Crump]], had intervened in a domestic dispute; in the process, Blake was tased and scuffled briefly with officers. He was shot as he tried to enter his SUV, where his children were in the backseat. The shooting was followed by protests, property damage, fires in downtown Kenosha, and further clashes with riot police. Commentators{{Who|date=August 2020}} drew links between the Blake shooting, the reaction, and the larger [[Black Lives Matter]] movement which had seen a resurgence in the wake of high-profile police killings in 2020, including those of [[Killing of George Floyd|George Floyd]], [[Killing of Rayshard Brooks|Rayshard Brooks]], and [[Shooting of Breonna Taylor|Breonna Taylor]].

==Shooting==
Police responded to a 911 call in which the dispatcher "reported that a complainant said Blake wasn't supposed to be there, and that he had taken the complainant's keys and refused to give them back".<ref name="Freiman">{{cite news | last1 = Freiman | first1 = Jordan| last2 = Carissimo | first2 = Justin | date = August 25, 2020 | title = Police in Wisconsin Shoot Black Man in Back Multiple Times, Sparking Protests | url = https://www.cbsnews.com/news/jacob-blake-kenosha-shooting-wisconsin-police-black-man-protesters-gather/ | work = CBS News}}</ref>  The police subsequently attempted to subdue Blake with a [[taser]].<ref name="thewashingtonpost"/><ref name="npr"/>  A bystander who heard police yelling "drop the knife" recorded a video which showed Blake surrounded by police with their weapons drawn.<ref>{{cite news | last = Pramuk | first = Jacob | date = August 24, 2020 | title = Joe Biden Says Kenosha Cops must be held accountable’ for shooting Black man Jacob Blake in the back | url = https://www.cnbc.com/2020/08/24/jacob-blake-joe-biden-calls-for-probe-of-kenosha-police-shooting.html | work = [[CNBC]] |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Blake was reportedly unarmed.<ref name=bbc/> Blake walked around his vehicle, and while attempting to enter it, one police officer grabbed him and fired his weapon seven times towards Blake's back.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2020/08/23/kenosha-police-shooting-video-wisconsin/|title=After video shows Wisconsin police shooting a Black man multiple times, National Guard is called to Kenosha|first1=Jaclyn|last1=Peiser|website=Washington Post}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Flores |first=Terry |date=August 23, 2020 |title=WATCH NOW: State DOJ Will Probe Officer-Involved Shooting; Man In Serious Condition |url=https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/local/watch-now-neighbors-man-shot-by-police-at-40th-street-and-28th-ave/article_f1adbd13-3a15-5cdd-bd4e-95113ca36de4.html |work=[[Kenosha News]] |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Blake's attorney, [[Benjamin Crump]], said Blake "was attempting to de-escalate a fight between two other people when officers arrived at the scene",<ref name="Freiman"/> and that Blake tried to enter his vehicle to check on his three sons inside.<ref>{{cite news |first=Alta |last=Spells |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2020/08/24/us/kenosha-police-shooting-jacob-blake/index.html |title=Wisconsin police shoot Black man as children watch from a car, attorney says |date=August 24, 2020 |publisher=[[CNN]] |department=US |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Blake was flown to [[Froedtert Hospital]].<ref name="npr">{{cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/08/24/905316709/wisconsin-police-shooting-leaves-black-man-in-serious-condition |title=Wisconsin Police Shooting Leaves Black Man In 'Serious Condition' |date=August 24, 2020 |publisher=[[NPR]] |first=Emma |last=Bowman |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> His father announced on August 25 that Blake was paralyzed from the waist down, and that doctors do not know if the injury will be permanent.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://chicago.suntimes.com/2020/8/25/21400481/jacob-blake-kenosha-police-shooting-riots-evanston |title=Jacob Blake’s father says son’s paralyzed from waist down after police shooting in Kenosha |date=August 24, 2020 |first=Clare |last=Proctor |publisher=[[Chicago Sun-Times]]}}</ref>

==Investigation==
The State Justice Department will investigate the shooting, with the findings going to District Attorney Michael D. Graveley. His office will determine whether or not to bring charges against the officers. Graveley stated that the investigation was in "its earliest stages."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2020/08/24/us/raysean-white-witness-jacob-blake-shooting/index.html|title=Man who recorded Jacob Blake's shooting says he's traumatized and cannot sleep|first=Amir Vera, Sarah Jorgensen and Polo Sandoval|last=CNN|website=CNN}}</ref> 

The family of Jacob Blake has retained attorney Benjamin Crump, who also represented the families of [[Shooting of Trayvon Martin|Trayvon Martin]] and [[Shooting of Michael Brown|Michael Brown]] in their civil suits.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/local/crime-and-courts/jacob-blakes-family-has-retained-prominent-civil-rights-attorney-ben-crump/article_d8455df7-34ce-5d3e-8571-82b3ce4432c0.html |title=Jacob Blake's family has retained prominent Civil Rights Attorney Ben Crump |date=August 24, 2020 |first=Adam |last=Rogan|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref>

==Protests==
{{further|Kenosha riot}}
Protests followed, leading the [[Kenosha County, Wisconsin|Kenosha County]] government to declare a [[State of emergency#United States|state of emergency]] overnight on August 24 after police vehicles were damaged, a dump truck was set on fire, and the local courthouse was vandalized.  One officer was knocked unconscious and transported to a hospital after being hit in the head with a brick, and tear gas was deployed.<ref name="npr"/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2020/aug/24/protests-in-wisconsin-after-video-appears-to-show-police-shooting-black-man-in-the-back |title=Protests in Wisconsin after video appears to show police shooting black man in the back|date=August 24, 2020|publisher=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | last = Palmer | first = Ewan | date = August 24, 2020 | title = Rioting in Kenosha Over Jacob Blake Shooting, State of Emergency Curfew Declared | url = https://www.newsweek.com/jacob-blake-police-shooting-riots-kenosha-1527086 | work = [[Newsweek]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://jsonline.com/story/news/2020/08/24/kenosha-protests-escalate-after-police-shoot-black-man-jacob-blake/3427941001/ |title=Several fires burning in Kenosha as unrest continues after police officer shoots Black man |date=August 24, 2020|publisher= [[Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Police urged 24-hour businesses to consider closing because of what it alleged were "numerous" calls about armed robberies and shots being fired.<ref name=bbc/> Wisconsin Governor [[Tony Evers]] deployed the [[Wisconsin National Guard]] to maintain public safety after the shooting. Up to 200 members will be deployed.<ref>{{cite web |title=National Guard deployed after US police shooting |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-53897641 |website=[[BBC News]] |date=24 August 2020 |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> On August 24th there rioters and protestors set fires and looted business for a second night.<ref name="KN">{{cite news |last1=Flores |first1=Terry |title=Authorities respond to multiple fires, vandalism and looting as Kenosha reels from Blake shooting |url=https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/authorities-respond-to-multiple-fires-vandalism-and-looting-as-kenosha-reels-from-blake-shooting/article_e196f328-a228-567f-8de1-764e4fb1b779.html |accessdate=25 August 2020 |publisher=Kenosha News |date=25 August 2020}}</ref><ref name="photos">{{cite news |last1=Flores |first1=Terry |title=in Photos: Rioters set fires in Kenosha |url=https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/local/in-photos-rioters-set-fires-in-kenosha/collection_9ec8affb-3aa1-5415-a66c-534e068909ad.html#1 |accessdate=25 August 2020 |publisher=Kenosha News |date=24 August 2020}}</ref> 

==Reactions==
===In Wisconsin===
Wisconsin Governor [[Tony Evers]] issued a statement denouncing the [[Police brutality in the United States|excessive use of force by police]] and invoking the names of African Americans killed by law enforcement.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cbs58.com/news/gov-evers-releases-statement-on-shooting-of-jacob-blake-in-kenosha |title=Gov. Evers releases statement on shooting of Jacob Blake in Kenosha |date=August 23, 2020| publisher=[[WDJT-TV]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Evers said, "While we do not have all of the details yet, what we know for certain is that he is not the first Black man or person to have been shot or injured or mercilessly killed at the hands of individuals in law enforcement in our state or our country."<ref name=bbc>{{cite news|url= https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-53886070|work=[[BBC News]]|department=US & Canada|title=Kenosha shooting: Protests erupt after US police shoot black man|date=August 24, 2020|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> 

Evers called Wisconsin state lawmakers into a special session to pass legislation addressing police brutality.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Beck |first=Molly |date=24 August 2020 |title= Tony Evers calls special session on reducing police brutality after shooting of Jacob Blake |work=[[Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]] |url=https://www.jsonline.com/story/news/politics/2020/08/24/wisconsin-governor-evers-calls-special-session-police-brutality-after-jacob-blake-shooting-kenosha/3430490001/ |access-date=24 August 2020}}</ref> 

===Elsewhere===
Presidential candidate [[Joe Biden]] said, "these shots pierce the soul of our nation".<ref>{{cite news | last = Radcliff | first = J.R. | date = August 24, 2020 | title = Prominent Figures React to the Jacob Blake Shooting in Kenosha, Including Joe Biden | url = https://www.jsonline.com/story/news/2020/08/24/prominent-figures-react-jacob-blake-shooting-kenosha/3428435001/ | work = [[Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref>
