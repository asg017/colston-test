'''Jacob S. Blake''', a 29-year-old [[African Americans|African-American]] man, was shot by Rusten Sheskey,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-26|title=DOJ identifies officer who shot Jacob Blake as Rusten Sheskey; says Blake had knife|url=https://www.fox6now.com/news/doj-identifies-officer-who-shot-jacob-blake-as-rusten-sheskey-says-blake-had-knife|access-date=2020-08-27|website=FOX 6 Now Milwaukee|language=en-US}}</ref> a [[Kenosha, Wisconsin|Kenosha]] police officer, on August 23, 2020, in the U.S. state of [[Wisconsin]] after Blake opened the door to his SUV and leaned into the vehicle.<ref name="AP">{{cite web |last1=Goldman |first1=David |title=17-year-old arrested after 2 killed during unrest in Kenosha|url=https://hosted.ap.org/standardspeaker/article/97a0700564fb52d7f664d8de22066f88/kenosha-police-3-shot-2-fatally-during-wisconsin-protests|publisher=[[AP]] |date=August 24, 2020|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref name=PeiserWP>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2020/08/23/kenosha-police-shooting-video-wisconsin/|title=After video shows Wisconsin police shooting a Black man multiple times, National Guard is called to Kenosha|first1=Jaclyn|last1=Peiser|first2=Mark|last2=Berman|first3=Mark|last3=Guarino|first4=Paulina|last4=Firozi|first5=Griff|last5=Witte|work=Washington Post|date=August 24, 2020|accessdate=August 26, 2020|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20200825040228/https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2020/08/23/kenosha-police-shooting-video-wisconsin/|archivedate=August 25, 2020}}</ref> Police were attempting to arrest Blake over a domestic violence dispute,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Report: Veteran NBA Player Says Remainder of Postseason ‘in Jeopardy'|url=https://www.nbcwashington.com/news/sports/nbcsports/report-veteran-nba-player-says-remainder-of-postseason-in-jeopardy/2402369/|access-date=2020-08-26|website=NBC4 Washington|language=en-US}}</ref> during which Blake was stun-gunned and scuffled with officers. He was shot after he opened the SUV driver's seat door as he leaned into the vehicle. During the encounter, three of Blake's sons were in the backseat of the SUV.

The shooting was followed by protests, property damage, fires in downtown Kenosha, and further clashes with riot police. Blake's name was invoked in protests in other cities as part of the larger [[Black Lives Matter]] movement, which had seen a resurgence in the wake of high-profile police killings in 2020.<ref name=PeiserWP />

==Shooting==
Kenosha police responded to a [[9-1-1]] call in which the dispatcher "reported that a complainant said Blake wasn't supposed to be there, and that he had taken the complainant's keys and refused to give them back".<ref name="Freiman">{{cite news | last1 = Freiman | first1 = Jordan| last2 = Carissimo | first2 = Justin | date = August 25, 2020 | title = Police in Wisconsin Shoot Black Man in Back Multiple Times, Sparking Protests | url = https://www.cbsnews.com/news/jacob-blake-kenosha-shooting-wisconsin-police-black-man-protesters-gather/ | work = CBS News}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | date = August 25, 2020 | title = Blake was breaking up a fight before he was shot, attorney says | url = https://www.cnn.com/us/live-news/jacob-blake-kenosha-wisconsin-shooting-08-25-2020/index.html | work = [[CNN]] |accessdate=August 25, 2020}}</ref> According to a witness, Blake had pulled his car up near "six or seven women shouting at each other on the sidewalk" and "Blake did not say anything to the women".<ref>{{cite news | date = August 24, 2020 | title = Kenosha, Wisconsin Shooting: Police Shoot Black Man From Behind; Protests Erupt With State Of Emergency Declared | url = https://abc7news.com/protest-erupts-after-wisconsin-police-shoot-black-man/6385663/ | work = [[KABC-TV|ABC7]] |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref>  Blake's attorney, [[Benjamin Crump]], said Blake "was attempting to de-escalate a fight between two other people when officers arrived at the scene",<ref name="Freiman"/> and that Blake tried to enter his vehicle to check on his three sons inside.<ref>{{cite news |first=Alta |last=Spells |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2020/08/24/us/kenosha-police-shooting-jacob-blake/index.html |title=Wisconsin police shoot Black man as children watch from a car, attorney says |date=August 24, 2020 |publisher=[[CNN]] |department=US |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref>

Officers attempted to subdue Blake, and [[taser|tased]] him.<ref name="thewashingtonpost">{{cite web |last1=Peiser |first1=Jaclyn |title=Video shows Wisconsin police shooting a Black man multiple times as he enters a car |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2020/08/23/kenosha-police-shooting-video-wisconsin/ |publisher=[[The Washington Post]] |date=August 24, 2020 |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref name="npr"/>  A bystander who recorded a video of the incident heard police yelling "drop the knife", and also said, "I didn't see any weapons in his hands, he wasn't being violent".<ref>{{cite news | last = Pramuk | first = Jacob | date = August 24, 2020 | title = Joe Biden Says Kenosha Cops must be held accountable’ for shooting Black man Jacob Blake in the back | url = https://www.cnbc.com/2020/08/24/jacob-blake-joe-biden-calls-for-probe-of-kenosha-police-shooting.html | work = [[CNBC]] |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref>  Other reports said Blake was unarmed.<ref name=bbc/>  After an initial scuffle, Blake got away and walked to the driver side of his vehicle followed by two officers with guns drawn.  The closest officer tried to grab Blake, and when Blake opened the door and leaned in, that officer grabbed him and fired at least seven shots into Blake's back.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2020/08/23/kenosha-police-shooting-video-wisconsin/|title=After video shows Wisconsin police shooting a Black man multiple times, National Guard is called to Kenosha|first1=Jaclyn|last1=Peiser|website=Washington Post}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Flores |first=Terry |date=August 23, 2020 |title=WATCH NOW: State DOJ Will Probe Officer-Involved Shooting; Man In Serious Condition |url=https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/local/watch-now-neighbors-man-shot-by-police-at-40th-street-and-28th-ave/article_f1adbd13-3a15-5cdd-bd4e-95113ca36de4.html |work=[[Kenosha News]] |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Crump said Blake was hit by four of the seven bullets.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sbs.com.au/news/jacob-blake-s-mother-calls-for-calm-on-third-day-of-protests-over-police-shooting-that-left-him-paralysed|title=Jacob Blake’s mother calls for calm on third day of protests over police shooting that left him paralysed|website=SBS News}}</ref> The Wisconsin Attorney General [[Josh Kaul]] held a press conference on August 26, 2020 to say that a knife was recovered on the driver's side floorboard of the Blake's vehicle, and Blake admitted to investigators that he had a knife.<ref name="Milw">{{cite news |title=DOJ: Kenosha Officer Rusten Sheskey fired weapon into Jacob Blake's back 7 times |url=https://cbs58.com/news/ag-kaul-kenosha-officer-rusten-sheskey-fired-weapon-into-jacob-blakes-back-7-times?fbclid=IwAR1duSCBkBJvKXgnXJLBklRVQHiY3b_BWu6V6jvT_QAP3s_1jd7RvjLAVpE |accessdate=26 August 2020 |agency=CBS |publisher=WDJT Milwaukee |date=26 August 2020}}</ref> 

Blake was flown to [[Froedtert Hospital]] in Wauwatosa, Wisconsin.<ref name="npr">{{cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/08/24/905316709/wisconsin-police-shooting-leaves-black-man-in-serious-condition |title=Wisconsin Police Shooting Leaves Black Man In 'Serious Condition' |date=August 24, 2020 |publisher=[[NPR]] |first=Emma |last=Bowman |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> His father announced on August 25 that Blake was paralyzed from the waist down and that doctors do not know if the injury will be permanent.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://chicago.suntimes.com/2020/8/25/21400481/jacob-blake-kenosha-police-shooting-riots-evanston |title=Jacob Blake’s father says son’s paralyzed from waist down after police shooting in Kenosha |date=August 24, 2020 |first=Clare |last=Proctor |publisher=[[Chicago Sun-Times]]}}</ref>

==Investigation and legal process==
Kenosha police referred the investigation of the shooting to the Wisconsin Division of Criminal Investigation. As part of the process, findings from the investigation will go to District Attorney Michael D. Graveley, the local official responsible for deciding to bring charges against the officers. Graveley stated on August 25 that the investigation was in "its earliest stages".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2020/08/24/us/raysean-white-witness-jacob-blake-shooting/index.html|title=Man who recorded Jacob Blake's shooting says he's traumatized and cannot sleep|first=Amir Vera, Sarah Jorgensen and Polo Sandoval|last=CNN|website=CNN}}</ref> On August 25, the [[United States Department of Justice]] also announced an investigation into the shooting. The investigation will look into whether Blake's civil rights were violated.<ref name="FedInvestigate">{{cite news|last1=Campbell|first1=Josh|date=25 August 2020|title=Federal investigators reviewing police shooting of Jacob Blake|publisher=CNN|url=https://www.cnn.com/us/live-news/jacob-blake-kenosha-wisconsin-shooting-08-25-2020/index.html|accessdate=25 August 2020}}</ref>

Blake's family retained Crump, who also represented the families of [[Shooting of Trayvon Martin|Trayvon Martin]] and [[Shooting of Michael Brown|Michael Brown]] in their civil suits.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/local/crime-and-courts/jacob-blakes-family-has-retained-prominent-civil-rights-attorney-ben-crump/article_d8455df7-34ce-5d3e-8571-82b3ce4432c0.html |title=Jacob Blake's family has retained prominent Civil Rights Attorney Ben Crump |date=August 24, 2020 |first=Adam |last=Rogan|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Crump has called for the officer who opened fire to be arrested and others involved to lose their jobs, and also said that it would "take a miracle" for Blake to walk again.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Householder|first=Mike|last2=Bauer|first2=Scott|date=2020-08-25|title=Lawyer: Blake not likely to walk again after shot by police|url=https://apnews.com/62ddb4c23511f0c1959fb4a691a81927|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-08-25|website=AP NEWS}}</ref>

On August 26, Wisconsin's attorney general Josh Kaul announced that Kenosha police officer Rusten Sheskey had shot Blake seven times in the back as he opened the door of his car. No other officers at the scene fired, but all have now been placed on administrative leave.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-08-27|title=Police officer named in Jacob Blake shooting|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-53927756|access-date=2020-08-27}}</ref>

==Protests==
{{further|Kenosha riot}}
Protests followed, leading the [[Kenosha County, Wisconsin|Kenosha County]] government to declare a [[State of emergency#United States|state of emergency]] overnight on August 24 after police vehicles were damaged, a dump truck was set on fire, and the local courthouse was vandalized.  One officer was knocked unconscious and transported to a hospital after being hit in the head with a brick, and tear gas was deployed.<ref name="npr"/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2020/aug/24/protests-in-wisconsin-after-video-appears-to-show-police-shooting-black-man-in-the-back |title=Protests in Wisconsin after video appears to show police shooting black man in the back|date=August 24, 2020|publisher=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://jsonline.com/story/news/2020/08/24/kenosha-protests-escalate-after-police-shoot-black-man-jacob-blake/3427941001/ |title=Several fires burning in Kenosha as unrest continues after police officer shoots Black man |date=August 24, 2020|publisher= [[Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Police urged 24-hour businesses to consider closing because of what it alleged were "numerous" calls about armed robberies and shots being fired.<ref name=bbc/> Wisconsin Governor [[Tony Evers]] deployed the [[Wisconsin National Guard]] to maintain public safety after the shooting. Up to 200 members were planned to be deployed.<ref>{{cite web |title=National Guard deployed after US police shooting |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-53897641 |website=[[BBC News]] |date=24 August 2020 |accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> On August 24, the protestors set fires and looted businesses for a second night.<ref name="KN">{{cite news |last1=Flores |first1=Terry |title=Authorities respond to multiple fires, vandalism and looting as Kenosha reels from Blake shooting |url=https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/authorities-respond-to-multiple-fires-vandalism-and-looting-as-kenosha-reels-from-blake-shooting/article_e196f328-a228-567f-8de1-764e4fb1b779.html |accessdate=25 August 2020 |publisher=Kenosha News |date=25 August 2020}}</ref><ref name="photos">{{cite news |last1=Flores |first1=Terry |title=in Photos: Rioters set fires in Kenosha |url=https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/local/in-photos-rioters-set-fires-in-kenosha/collection_9ec8affb-3aa1-5415-a66c-534e068909ad.html#1 |accessdate=25 August 2020 |publisher=Kenosha News |date=24 August 2020}}</ref> On August 26, 2020 the protests and fires continued throughout Kenosha. Citizens armed with long guns stood guard in parts of the city and at some point several protestors were shot.<ref name="Guard">{{cite news |last1=Becket |first1=Louis |last2=Wong |first2=Charlie |last3=Bekiempis |first3=Victoria |title=Armed white men patrolling Kenosha protests organized on Facebook |url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2020/aug/26/kenosha-militia-protest-shooting-facebook |accessdate=27 August 2020 |publisher=Guardian News & Media Limited |date=26 August 2020}}</ref> A video showed a young white man initially shooting another man who pursued him into a car parking lot and threw something at him.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/nation/2020/08/26/kenosha-shooting-shots-fired-during-protest-injuries-reported/3441475001/|title=2 shot dead and 1 injured in Kenosha during protests; police looking for man armed with a long gun|first=Patrick Marley and Sarah|last=Volpenhein|website=USA TODAY|accessdate=August 27, 2020}}</ref>

==Reactions==
===Wisconsin===
Wisconsin Governor [[Tony Evers]] issued a statement denouncing the [[Police brutality in the United States|excessive use of force by police]] and invoking the names of African Americans killed by law enforcement.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cbs58.com/news/gov-evers-releases-statement-on-shooting-of-jacob-blake-in-kenosha |title=Gov. Evers releases statement on shooting of Jacob Blake in Kenosha |date=August 23, 2020| publisher=[[WDJT-TV]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Evers said, "While we do not have all of the details yet, what we know for certain is that he is not the first Black man or person to have been shot or injured or mercilessly killed at the hands of individuals in law enforcement in our state or our country."<ref name=bbc>{{cite news|url= https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-53886070|work=[[BBC News]]|department=US & Canada|title=Kenosha shooting: Protests erupt after US police shoot black man|date=August 24, 2020|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref> 

Evers called Wisconsin state lawmakers into a special session to pass legislation addressing police brutality.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Beck |first=Molly |date=24 August 2020 |title= Tony Evers calls special session on reducing police brutality after shooting of Jacob Blake |work=[[Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]] |url=https://www.jsonline.com/story/news/politics/2020/08/24/wisconsin-governor-evers-calls-special-session-police-brutality-after-jacob-blake-shooting-kenosha/3430490001/ |access-date=24 August 2020}}</ref>

===Elsewhere===
Former Vice President and 2020 Democratic presidential nominee [[Joe Biden]] said, "these shots pierce the soul of our nation".<ref>{{cite news | last = Radcliff | first = J.R. | date = August 24, 2020 | title = Prominent Figures React to the Jacob Blake Shooting in Kenosha, Including Joe Biden | url = https://www.jsonline.com/story/news/2020/08/24/prominent-figures-react-jacob-blake-shooting-kenosha/3428435001/ | work = [[Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]]|accessdate=August 24, 2020}}</ref>

On August 24, in [[New York City]] 700 protesters marched from [[Times Square]] to Brooklyn to condemn the shooting and calls for [[Defund the police|defunding the police]]. Metal barriers were erected to prevent protesters from crossing the [[Brooklyn Bridge]]; however, 300 protesters jumped them.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Burke|first=Kerry|date=2020-08-24|title=‘It keeps happening’: Hundreds of protesters fill Manhattan streets to condemn shooting of Wisconsin Black man in front of his children|url=https://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/ny-protest-blm-jacob-blake-20200825-vwkinxjg7jew5kssjexy2eevd4-story.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-08-26|website=NY Daily News}}</ref>

===Sports===

In the [[2020 NBA Bubble|NBA Bubble]], the [[2019–20 Milwaukee Bucks season|Milwaukee Bucks]] boycotted their August 26 first-round playoff game against the [[2019–20 Orlando Magic season|Orlando Magic]] in protest of the shooting. The team decided not to come out of their locker room as the Magic were on the court warming up minutes before the scheduled start to the game.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nba/story/_/id/29747523/bucks-not-taking-court-game-5-vs-magic|title=Bucks boycotting Game 5 vs. Magic, source says|work=ESPN|date=August 26, 2020}}</ref> The [[2019–20 Toronto Raptors season|Toronto Raptors]] have also discussed a boycott of their second round playoff series with the [[2019–20 Boston Celtics season|Boston Celtics]] in frustration with a lack of social or legislative change after [[Killing of George Floyd|George Floyd's death]] and as a result of Blake's shooting.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/nba/article/emotional-frustrated-raptors-discussed-boycott-wake-jacob-blake-shooting/|title=Emotional, frustrated Raptors have discussed boycott in wake of Jacob Blake shooting|last=Grange|first=Michael|date=25 August 2020|publisher=[[Sportsnet]]|accessdate=26 August 2020}}</ref> Later that day, the NBA and the [[National Basketball Players Association]] announced that in light of the Milwaukee Bucks’ decision to refuse to play, all NBA games for the day were postponed.<ref>{{Cite web|last=release|first=Official|title=NBA postpones playoff games|url=http://www.nba.com/article/2020/08/26/nba-playoff-games-postponed|access-date=2020-08-26|website=NBA.com|language=en}}</ref>

On August 26, The [[Milwaukee Brewers]] and [[Cincinnati Reds]] become the first teams in [[Major League Baseball]] to boycott a game in protest of the shooting of Jacob Blake.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Williams |first1=Rob |title=Brewers and Reds become first MLB teams to boycott game for Jacob Blake |url=https://dailyhive.com/vancouver/brewers-and-reds-become-first-mlb-teams-to-boycott-game-for-jacob-blake |website=Daily Hive}}</ref>
